Premier League

Introduction

In the early 1990s, English First Division clubs sought a radical restructuring of football to ensure growth and success. On 17 July 1991, they signed the Founder Members Agreement, laying the foundation for the Premier League. The league was granted commercial independence from the Football League and The FA, allowing it to manage its own broadcasting and sponsorship deals.

On 20 February 1992, all 22 First Division clubs resigned from the Football League, and on 27 May, the Premier League was officially established as a limited company. A groundbreaking television deal was signed with BSkyB, which played a crucial role in the league’s development.

The first Premier League season began on 15 August 1992, with 22 teams. Among them, Arsenal, Chelsea, Everton, Liverpool, Manchester United, and Tottenham Hotspur have remained ever-present. To enhance competitiveness and quality, the league reduced the number of teams to 20 by the end of the 1994/95 season.
